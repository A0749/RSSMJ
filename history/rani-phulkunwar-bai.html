The Story of Rani Phulkunwar Bai

RAJA SHANKAR SHAH
KUNWAR RAGHUNATH SHAH
TRIBAL FREEDOM FIGHTERS MUSEUM
↓

Rani Phul Kunwar Bai stands as a remarkable figure in the history of resistance against British colonial rule in India. As the wife of Raja Shankar Shah and the mother of Raghunath Shah, her legacy is deeply intertwined with the struggles of her family and the Gond community in Garha Mandla.

A fierce warrior, Rani Phul Kunwar is celebrated locally for her courageous opposition to the East India Company. Her valiant efforts in defending her homeland and her people have become legendary, with many Gond communities commemorating her life through songs that recount not only her grand wedding ceremony but also her unwavering fight against British oppression.

Following the deaths of her husband and son, Rani Phul Kunwar displayed immense strength and resolve. On September 18, 1857, she gathered the scattered remains of Raja Shankar Shah and Raghunath Shah, performing the proper death rites for them. This act was a profound demonstration of her love and respect for her family and their sacrifices in the struggle for freedom.

In the aftermath of Raja Shankar Shah's death, Rani Phul Kunwar seized control of the fort of Ramgarh, leading her forces in several skirmishes against the British. However, the relentless pursuit by British forces eventually forced her into a dire situation. In a poignant act of defiance and honor, she dismounted from her horse and plunged a sword into her bosom, mirroring the courageous actions of her great ancestor, Rani Durgavati, who also fought valiantly to protect her kingdom.
