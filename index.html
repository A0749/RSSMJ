About the Museum

Raja Shankar Shah and Kunwar Raghunath Shah, hailing from the lineage of King Nizam Shah of the Gond Kingdom, emerged as prominent figures in the resistance against British colonial rule during the 1857 uprising. Despite advocating for non-violence, they wielded their poetic prowess as a potent tool to oppose British hegemony. Their unwavering commitment to the cause led to their arrest on September 14, 1857, followed by their execution on September 18, 1857, at the hands of British authorities. Their sacrifice, etched into the annals of Indian history, remains a testament to the indomitable spirit of resistance against colonial rule. In recognition of their exceptional valor and sacrifice, the Tribal Affairs Department Government of Madhya Pradesh, in collaboration with the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, Government of India, took a significant step by dedicating a museum-cum-memorial in their memory in 2021. This museum, spread over one acre, was established at a cost of ₹14.26 crore, with INTACH entrusted with building restoration, museum design and curation work.

Gallery 1

Ethos of Gondwana

The gallery presents the vibrant history, tradition and culture of the Gondwana tribe. Inspired by the colorful Gond art and music, the gallery showcases vivid artworks highlighting the Royal Gondwana sigil featuring lion hunting an elephant, sacred symbols like the sun and moon honoring nature’s eternal presence, sociocultural, religious and agricultural practices and the tribe’s close relationship with nature. The gallery also showcases multiple artefacts providing an insight of the day-to-day life of the Gondwana tribe along with a map illustrating the vast Gondwana kingdom

Gallery 2

Echoes of Freedom: The 1857 Uprising

The gallery commemorates the heroic struggle of the first war of Independence, encapsulating the essence of the mutiny’s inception, the valor of martyrs, and pivotal events. It features illustrated stories of martyrs like Dhian Singh, Dhillanshah Gond, Bhima Naik, Gadhadhar Tiwari, Khajya Nayak, Meherban Singh alias Jangi Raja, Nawab Fazil Mohammad Khan, and Tantya Bhil alias Mama.

Additionally, the gallery pays homage to the tribal freedom fighters of Madhya Pradesh. A short documentary highlighting the freedom struggle and sacrifices, particularly in the context of Madhya Pradesh, is also on display. This documentary serves as a tribute to the courage and resilience of those who fought tirelessly for India’s independence.

Gallery 3

Flames of Courage: Raja and Kunwar’s Martyrdom

A Darbar Hall of Raja Shankar Shah has been created in the gallery which is inspired by Gond forts and palaces. The struggle and sacrifice of Raja Shankar Shah and Kunwar Raghunath Shah is depicted through an animated projection mapping show. The projection on walls and floor synchronized with sound and visuals, create an immersive experience.

Gallery 4

Defiance and Loyalty: The 52nd Regiment & The Resolute Queens

The gallery is dedicated to honoring the sacrifice of the esteemed queens of the region - Rani Phulkunwar Bai, Rani Mankunwar Bai, and Rani Avanti Bai - along with the 52nd Bengal Native Regiment, who bravely stood against the British following the execution of Raja and Kunwar. A map illustrating the movement of the 52nd regiment, along with major events, is prominently displayed in the gallery.

Furthermore, the gallery pays homage to the martyrs of the Jabalpur region. A short, animated documentary highlighting their valor and sacrifice is showcased, providing visitors with a deeper insight into the local heroes who played a crucial role in India’s fight for independence.

Gallery 5

Eternal Tribute: Raja and Kunwar’s Legacy

Two holograms with interactive panels have been installed for visitors to pay their respects to Raja Shankar Shah and Kunwar Raghunath Shah. The revolutionary poem written by Raja Shankar Shah is displayed within the gallery, showcasing his spirit and dedication to the cause of freedom.

As a symbol of British cruelty and the martyrdom of Raja Shankar Shah and Kunwar Raghunath Shah, a model of a cannon has been displayed in the museum. This representation as a medium to illustrate the oppressive tactics employed by the British and the ultimate sacrifice made by the courageous leaders in the fight for the Independence of our motherland.

Gallery 6

Memorial

There is a stone structure on the western side of the museum building, referred to as “Bandi Grah”. Within these weathered walls, Raja Shankar Shah and Kunwar Raghunath Shah endured four days of unjust captivity before their tragic execution by the British.

In tribute to their extraordinary sacrifice for the nation’s freedom, a solemn memorial graces the heart of the main building. At the rear end, a dignified podium hosts the sculpted busts of Raja and Kunwar. Here, amidst a hushed reverence, visitors may kindle candles and tenderly pay homage to these revered heroes, in memory of their unwavering courage and dedication to India’s struggle for independence.
