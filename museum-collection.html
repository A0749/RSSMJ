Bhura Bhagat

Bhura Bhagat is crafted from brass, and the Gond tribe venerates it during traditional religious ceremonies and special occasions.

Bhurabhagat(panchayat)

The bhurabhagat (panchayat) is made of brass and is worshiped by the Gond tribe of Chhindwara during traditional religious ceremonies.

Trishul

The trishul is made of iron and is crafted by the Lohar or Mandla's Agariya tribe. It is consecrated and worshiped in traditional ceremonies by the Gond tribe. This symbol is found in almost all communities.

Mangrohan Kham

The Mangrohan Kham is made from a single piece of wood and is crafted by carpenters, known as Sutaar or Badhayi, with intricate carvings sometimes added. The Gond tribe in Chhindwara uses the Mangrohan Kham for worship during marriage ceremonies.

Pajama

Pajama is made of white cotton fabric. It is tailored by a tailor. It is used by men of the Gond tribe in Mandla and Chhindwara for daily wear.

Churidar Pajama

Churidar pajama is made of white silk fabric. It is worn by men of the Gond tribe in Mandla on occasions such as weddings, festivals, and other celebrations.

Bana

The "Bana" is a musical instrument of the Gond tribe, specifically the Pardhan community. It is made from sagwan wood, leather, and bamboo. The Bana is associated with the worship of the major deity Bade Dev, and it is played during religious rituals, Teej, festivals, and other auspicious occasions.

Barooddani

A gunpowder flask is made from wood and is intricately carved using a chisel. It is used by the Gond tribe in Mandla to store gunpowder.

Chonkya(Tuntuna)

The musical instrument "Chonkya" is made from bamboo, wood, leather, and strings. It is used as a solo instrument by the Gond and Baiga tribes in Balaghat and Mandla for various occasions, including weddings and festivals. The musician plays this instrument, and in return, seeks compensation for their performance during events like weddings and other celebrations.

Fota

"Fota" is made of cotton fabric and has a yellow color. It is locally bought from the market. During Teej festivals or traditional dances, men from the Gond-Baiga tribes in Dindauri and Mandla wear it wrapped around their heads.

Gamcha

A "gamcha" is made of cotton fabric and has thin colorful borders on both edges. Men from the Gond tribe in Chindwada use it to drape over their shoulders or wrap around their heads.

Itarni

"Itarni" is made of brass metal. Its lower part is round, pendant-shaped, and the upper part is long and pointed, with three to four holes. The Gond tribal community in Mandla uses it for sprinkling scented water during auspicious occasions or weddings.

Kaadi Khochni

Kaadi Khochni is made of brass (metal). It has an artistic shape. The Gond tribal community in Mandla uses it during worship ceremonies for holding incense sticks.

Kandhvana

The musical instrument "Kandhvana" or "Chikada" is made from wood, leather, and bamboo. The Pardhan tribe plays it during weddings and other festive celebrations at Gond community events.

Khalta

Khalta bag jut ki rassi se bana hota hai. Iske dono siron me chamde ke lattu bane hote hai. Isme semal ki rui rakhi jati hai. Chindwada ki gond janjati dwara aak sugani ki samagri , chakmak pathar loh dhatu ki pattika ityadi rakhne ke liye iska upyog kiya jata hai.

Batua

"Pisu" or "Batua" is crafted by women using cut pieces of cotton and colorful fabrics. It has a rectangular shape and is used by women from the tribal communities in Chindwada and Mandla to keep items like money, lime, tobacco, etc.

Saarangi

The "Saarangi" is a musical instrument made from wood, leather, and strings. It is played during dance performances, weddings, and other festive occasions. The Pardhan sub-tribe of the Gond community is particularly known for playing this instrument.

Sendri Dhoti

A "Sendri Dhoti" is made from cotton fabric, is plain-colored, and has a wide border, making it heavy. It is crafted by weavers and is purchased from local markets for various occasions. Gond women from Chinwada and Mandla use it for festivals, weddings, fairs, and special events.

Tumba

A musical instrument called "tumba" is made from dried bottle gourd (lauki). The upper part of the dried gourd is cut off, seeds are removed, and it is cleaned for use. It is then used by various tribes like Bheel, Gond, Korku, Baiga, Madiya, etc., for holding water, storing chemicals like sulfur, and more.

Jewellery

Jewellery
Tora

Tora is a piece of jewellery made of Aluminium. It is made by intertwining aluminium wires. It is worn by women of the Gond tribe in regions of Chhindwara and Mandla in their daily lives.

Rigna

Ring/Rigna is a piece of jewellery made of iron. On the outer surface, the prominent design is mostly circular, like a rope. It is worn by the men of the Gond tribe in regions of Mandla in their daily lives.

Pahunchi

Pahunchi is made of nickel or thick cotton string. It is made by braiding of the strings. It is worn by women of the Gond tribe in regions of Chhindwara and Mandla in their daily lives.

Bali

Bali is a piece of jewellery made of brass or silver. It is round in shape, with a thick middle portion and thin, pointed ends on both sides. It is worn in ear by men of the Gond tribe in Mandla in their daily lives.

Kanosi

Kanosi is a jewellery made of silver, brass, or nickel. It has one thick end with designs and another thin, pointed end. It is used for wearing in the ears in various tribes such as Bhil, Gond, Korku and others.

Hansli

Hansli is a piece of jewellery made of nickel-mixed silver metal. It is hollow from the inside and has designs on the outer surface. It is used by women of the Gond tribe in Mandla for wearing around the neck.

Kakna

Kakna (Kangan) is a short silver bracelet. It is circular in shape with a flat upper surface adorned with designs. It is used by women of the Gond tribe in Mandla for wearing on their hands.

Kangan

Kangan is a jewellery made of brass metal. It is used by women of the Birhor tribe in Raigarh for wearing on their hands. This jewellery is worn in daily life.

Pajna

Pajna (or Payjna) is made of Nickel. It is used by women of the Gond tribe in Chhindwara and Mandla to adorn their daily lives by wearing it on the arms.

Ghunghru

The Ghunghru is made of brass metal. It is worn on the feet as a musical accessory, specifically used in Mandlas Gond tribe during dances like Karma, Saul, Faag, weddings etc.

Bogri

Bogri is a jewellery made of silver. It has a hollow interior and is adorned with designs on the outer surface. It is used by women of the Gond tribe in Mandla for wearing on the hands in their daily lives.

Bajubandh

Bajubandh is a jewellery made of silver. It is used by women of the Gond tribe in Chhindwara and Mandla to adorn their daily lives by wearing it on the arms.

Pata Saree

A "Pata Saree" is made from cotton fabric and is generally heavy. Gond women from Chinwada and Mandla use it for special occasions like Teej, weddings, Bela-Madai, and other festive events.

Pagdi

The "Pagdi" is made from a fabric called "kosa." It has a golden head and a patterned design. It is specifically used by men of the Gond tribe in Mandla for special occasions such as weddings, Teej festivals, and other celebrations to be worn on the head.

Chaadar

A "chaadar" is made from a fabric known as "sooti," and it features a checkered design. It is locally purchased from handloom weavers. It is prepared by weavers and is used by the Gond tribal community in Mandla to cover and decorate their homes.

Rajai

The "rajai" is made from printed "sooti" fabric that is sourced from local markets. In the Gond tribal communities of Chhindwara and Mandla, this "rajai" is used to cover and protect oneself by wrapping it around a quilt or blanket.

Batua

A "Batua" is a small cotton fabric pouch with a cotton string lock at the top for easy opening and closing. Colorful tassels (jhumkas) adorn both ends of the string. Gond women and men use it to carry essentials such as tobacco, lime, and currency notes. Women personally craft it using needle and thread.

Utensils & Household Items

Gangaar

Gangaar is a large vessel made of brass metal. It is used by the Gond tribal community in Mandla for storing water.

Jaam Gilas

The "Jaam gilas" is made of brass. It is beautiful and decorative. It is used by the Gond and Baiga tribal communities in Mandla for drinking water and consuming beverages.

Khurawale Gilas

The "Khurawale gilas" is made of bell metal. It has an expanding upper part and a spreading lower part. It is used by the Gond and Baiga tribal communities in Mandla for drinking water and consuming beverages.

Safeda Gilas

The "Safeda gilas" is made of stainless steel. It is used by the Gond and Baiga tribal communities in Mandla for drinking water or consuming beverages.

Gilas

The "gilas" is made of brass and bronze (metals). It is used by the Gond and Baiga tribal communities in Mandla for drinking water.

Gangajali

The "Gangajali" is made of brass (metal). It has a handle attached to the lid for easy handling. The Gond and Baiga tribal communities in Mandla use it to store holy water from the Ganges in their homes.

Mirzapur lota

The "Mirzapur lota" is a vessel made of bell metal (an alloy of copper and tin). It is used for domestic tasks by the Gond and Baiga tribal communities in Mandla.

Gadua

The "Gadua" is made of brass and bronze. It has a smaller size compared to other vessels and is relatively heavy. It is used during worship ceremonies by the Gond and Baiga tribal communities in Mandla.

Gola Lota

The "gola lota" is made of a mixture of brass and bronze. It has a spherical shape and does not have a handle. It is used for domestic and kitchen purposes by the Gond and Baiga tribal communities in Mandla.

Okhli

The "Okhli" grinding stone, made of durable pink-colored stone, is skillfully carved by traditional artisans into a square outer shape with intricate designs. Embedded in the ground, it is used by women from the Bhil and Bhilala tribes of Jhabua, along with a wooden pestle, to grind grains.

Nakki Degchi

The "nakki" is made of brass (metal). The Gond, Bhatra, Maadiya, Muriya tribal communities in Bastar and Mandla use it for heating water or cooking in the kitchen.

Kunda

The "kunda" is made of brass (metal). Its upper part is spread out like a plate, and the lower part is tapered. The Gond and Baiga tribal communities in Mandla use it for keeping flatbreads (roti).

Chhaaka

The "Chhaaka" is a brass (metal) utensil in the form of a bowl. The Gond and Baiga tribal communities in Mandla use it for drinking water or as a bowl.

Tatuar

"Tatuar" is a brass (metal) plate used in kitchen activities. It is utilized by the Gond and Baiga tribal communities in Mandla for culinary purposes.

Chunatui

The chunauti (mortar) is made of wood and comes in various shapes and sizes. It is adorned with artistic carvings and crafted by a skilled artisan. It is used by various tribes such as Gond, Maadiya, Muriya, Uraav, Korku, Baiga, etc., for keeping edible lime.

Saadsi

Saadsi is made of iron and is made by the Agariya tribe. It is used in the kitchen by the people of Agariya, Gond and Bega tribe for cooking and for melting iron and holding the tray in a kiln.

Kadchul

The kadchul (ladle) is made of brass. It is utilized in kitchen activities by the Gond, Baiga, and other tribes in Mandla.

Sancha

It is made from Sagwan wood. It is circular in shape. Its exterior surface is adorned with beautiful design. It is used to make puri or roti which is flat bread in festivals and occassions by the people of Bhil, Bhilala and Gond tribe.

Thakur Dev

Thakur Dev is made of brass, a metal alloy of copper and zinc. It is crafted by the Dhadwa tribe. The Gond community traditionally worships Thakur Dev during religious ceremonies and rituals.

Dulha Dev

Dulha Dev is a deity made of stone and is crafted by skilled artisans. The Gond tribe in Chhindwara specifically worships Dulha Dev during special occasions and wedding ceremonies.

Chutkule

Chutkule are made from wood. During dance performances, dancers hold them in their hands and perform. The Gond-Pardhan tribe in Mandla district uses them during occasions like Karma, Shail, Faag, Holi, etc., while dancing.

Manjeere

Manjeere are made from brass and bronze. The Gond tribe in Mandla district plays them during Bhajan- Kirtan and Ramsatta (ramdhun) occasions.

Jhanjh

The "Jhanjh" is made of brass (metal). It is played by the Gond, Baiga, and Bhil tribes during occasions like bhajan-kirtan and Ramsatta.

Churuk Dhana Khel

Churuk Dhana Khel is a prominent game played by the Gond tribe in the regions of Chhindwara, Balaghat, Dindori, and Mandla. It is prepared using two wooden pieces. A straight piece of wood with a hole is prepared, and another stick resembling a bow is attached to it.

Pittu

The game of Pittu is played with 12 pieces of clay (round-shaped fragments) and a ball. This game is played not only by the Gond tribe but also by other tribal communities.

Kinnari

The "Kinnari" (Iktara) is made from bamboo, gourd, and strings. Artists of the Gond tribe in Chhindwara district play it while going from house to house during wedding ceremonies. They play it and ask for donations, receiving grains and money as gifts.

Tools & Arms

Kasiya

A "kasiya" is made of wood and metal. It has a knife or dagger-like shape, and it is sharp and pointed. It is often kept in a wooden or leather sheath. The Gond tribal community in Mandla uses it for their protection or during weddings, where the groom hangs it around the waist like a ceremonial dagger.

Kharda

The "Kharda" is made of iron. Its upper part is stamped, strong, triangular, and pointed, while the lower part is round and polished, into which a wooden handle is fitted. It is used by the Gond tribal community of Mandla and Chhindwara for their protection.

Katna

A "katna" is made of wood and iron. It has a sharp edge and is crafted by ironworkers. The Gond tribe in Chhindwara uses it for forestry and agricultural activities.

Kangura

Kangura is made of iron. Its upper part is trident-shaped and pointed, while the lower part is round and polished, with a wooden handle attached. The Gond tribal community uses it for their protection.

Katna(Wood&Iron)

A "katna" is made of wood and iron. It has a sharp edge and is crafted by ironworkers. The Gond tribe in Chhindwara uses it for forestry and agricultural activities.

Naal

Naal is made of iron. It has a hollow inside and is quite heavy. It is crafted by specialized artisans. The Gond tribal community in Mandla uses it for protective purposes.

Talwar

A sword is made of iron. It is sharp, pointed, and has a blade that can be wide or narrow. It is used by tribal communities like Gond and Bheel for their protection.

Kavach

A "Kavach" is made of small iron plates joined together. It is crafted by specialized artisans. The Gond tribal community in Mandla uses it for protection in warfare.

Bharmaar Banduk

"Bharmaar Banduk" is made of wood and iron. It is used for personal protection by the Gond tribal community in Mandla.

Shyaam Laathi

A "Shyaam Laathi" is made of bamboo and is approximately 6 feet long. It has iron rings fitted in the upper part, providing convenience for striking. It is used for personal protection by the Gond tribal community in Mandla.

Kundi Anaaj Maapak

Kundi Anaaj Maapak is a measuring tool used for grains. It is crafted by carving a thick piece of wood, and its construction is typically carried out by a skilled artisan, often known as a "badhai" or a carpenter. In some areas, it is also known as kudo. This tool is specifically used by the Gond tribal community in Mandla for measuring grains.

Chidiya Chimney

The Chidiya Chimney is made of brass (metal) and has a shape resembling a bird (chidiya). The Gond tribal community in Chhindwada uses it for providing light in their homes during the night, typically with the use of a lamp.

Patli/Pera

It is made from slender grains. It has a round and flat shape. It is used by the Gond tribe for sitting.

Saam(Dainta)

This is made of a mixture of iron and brass. It is approximately 05 to 06 inches long, with a pointed upper part and a rounded lower part from the inside. It is fitted into wood. The Gond tribal community in Mandla uses it for their protection.

Teer

A "teer" is made by fitting a pointed iron head into the bamboo or horsewood shaft. It is prepared by a blacksmith or the Agariya tribe in Mandla. It is used by tribal communities such as Maadiya, Muriya, Bheel, Gond, Baiga, Bhilala for hunting purposes.

Tokni

A "Tokni" is made of bamboo. It is typically round in shape with a lid and a handle. The Gond tribal community in Mandla uses it to store betel leaves, betel nuts, lime, and other items.

Kodo

Kodo, a millet type, is widely cultivated by tribal communities in Madhya Pradesh, especially by Baiga and Gond tribes, who commonly use its flour to make thick and flavorful bread.

Kulathi

In Chhindwara, Gond and Bhariya communities play a significant role in cultivating Kulathi, known for its effectiveness in dissolving kidney stones.

Kutki

The Gond and Bhariya communities in Chhindwara cultivate Kutki as a food crop, cooking it like rice for warmth and energy. Pregnant women often consume it with medicinal herbs. Kutki is notably abundant in Chhindwara.

Saawaan

In the districts of Mandla and Dindori, the Baiga and Gond communities residing there cultivate Kulathi in their fields. It can also be grown on uncultivated or fallow land. Kulathi falls into the category of coarse grains and is known to be nutritious.

Khumri

Khumri" is made from bamboo and mahaul leaves. It has a conical or pentagon shape. The tribal communities like Maadiya, Baiga, and Gond use it during agricultural and forestry activities to protect themselves from rain.

Topla

A "Topla" is made of bamboo. It can have a round or square shape. The Gond tribal community in Mandla uses it for agricultural, forestry, and domestic purposes.
